BULL PIT
Game Rulebook and GM Guide
Version: March 30, 2026

======================================================================
1. GAME OVERVIEW
======================================================================

Bull Pit is a live team trading game built around an America-focused
macro storyline. Teams trade stocks, shorts, futures, options, and
defined-risk hedge packages across seven connected rounds.

Core format
- Up to 60 teams
- Starting capital: $100,000 per team
- Seven rounds with no capital reset
- Carry model: gains, losses, borrow cost, and funding drag carry forward
- Live leaderboard during play; final winner decided by locked scorecard

What makes this version different
- Disruptions are inference-driven. The player-facing disruption screen
  does not directly reveal the affected sectors or stocks.
- Teams must infer exposure from the macro bulletin, price action, news,
  order flow, and their own economic reasoning.
- After every round, each team gets a private 5-second derivative
  settlement window showing only that team's expired derivative results.


======================================================================
2. WIN CONDITION
======================================================================

The final winner is the team with the highest locked composite score
after Round 7. During the game, teams are ranked live by net equity, but
final placement uses the 105-point scorecard plus tie-breakers.


======================================================================
3. WHAT TEAMS CAN DO
======================================================================

Teams can:
- Buy stocks
- Sell long positions
- Short stocks
- Cover shorts
- Trade round-expiry futures
- Trade round-expiry calls and puts
- Use Hedge and Strategy Builder packages such as protective puts,
  collars, spreads, and iron condors when their buying power allows it
- Answer prediction market questions when the GM opens them

Important trade mechanics
- Long buys consume cash
- Short sales lock proceeds as restricted collateral
- Borrow and funding costs accumulate across rounds
- Trades fill with a spread that widens in stress rounds
- Margin rules tighten in later rounds


======================================================================
4. ROUND FLOW
======================================================================

Every round follows the same broad structure:

1. Buffer opener
- The next round begins with a full-screen macro bulletin.
- Players see the storyline, clues, and challenge question if one is
  attached.
- They do not see direct ticker or sector hints on this screen.

2. Team inference phase
- Teams interpret the bulletin and decide where the market impact is most
  likely to land.
- They should use price movement, news, order flow, and the round regime
  to confirm or reject their thesis.

3. Live trading round
- Teams trade throughout the round.
- Silent storyline beats may post into News mid-round without a full-screen
  interruption.

4. Round expiry and settlement
- At round end, contracts expiring that round are settled automatically.
- Each team sees a private 5-second settlement window showing:
  - contract label
  - long or short side
  - quantity
  - settlement price
  - realized value
  - net P&L
  - cash effect

5. Transition to next round
- After the settlement window, the normal inter-round buffer continues.
- The next macro bulletin then frames the incoming round.


======================================================================
5. DISRUPTIONS AND STORYLINE RULES
======================================================================

Player-facing disruptions are intentionally indirect.

What players do see
- Headline
- Macro summary
- Inference clues
- Optional challenge question

What players do not see
- Direct stock list
- Direct sector list
- Explicit "winners" and "losers" labels

GM principle
- Do not over-explain. Let teams do the inference work.
- The challenge is not reading ticker hints. The challenge is connecting
  macro cause to market consequence.

======================================================================
7. DERIVATIVES AND HEDGE PACKAGES
======================================================================

Every listed Bull Pit stock has round-expiry derivatives.

Available instruments
- Futures
- Calls
- Puts

General principles
- Futures settle to mark; P&L flows through cash
- Options settle to intrinsic value at expiry
- Long options can expire worthless
- Short options can create negative cash settlements

Hedge and Strategy Builder
- Protective Put
- Collar
- Vertical spreads
- Iron Condor

Execution rule
- Multi-leg hedge packages execute as a single server-side package, not as
  separate naked-leg trades.
- Defined-risk packages use package-level risk for validation.

Settlement window rule
- After each round, the team-specific 5-second settlement panel appears
  before the normal next-round disruption view.
- This panel is private to each team.


======================================================================
8. MARGIN, BORROW, AND FUNDING
======================================================================

Short proceeds
- Cash raised from shorting is restricted collateral, not free cash.

Margin requirement
- Each round has its own initial and maintenance margin.
- Buying power depends on net equity and gross exposure.

Margin call
- If net equity falls below maintenance margin, a warning window begins.
- Grace period: 12 seconds

Auto-deleveraging
- If the team does not restore maintenance margin in time, the game closes
  the largest gross exposures first until the account is compliant again.

Carry model
- There is no forced equal-cash restart between rounds.
- Borrow cost and funding drag continue to compound.


======================================================================
9. PREDICTION MARKET
======================================================================

At selected times, the GM can open a prediction question tied to the
round's macro concept.

Rules
- Teams answer once per question
- Correct answers earn a capital bonus
- The feature rewards economic reasoning, not speed alone

GM guidance
- Open the prediction market during the buffer when you want teams engaged
  but not yet trading
- Keep the reveal clean and brief


======================================================================
10. POWER-UP CARDS
======================================================================

The GM has five one-time-use intervention cards.

1. Tsunami
- Entire market crashes 15% instantly

2. Moon Shot
- One random stock surges 40% for 60 seconds

3. Circuit Breaker
- Market-wide halt for 45 seconds

4. Sector Rotation
- One sector rallies 12% while another drops 12%

5. Political Shock
- Fires a macro-economic political event mid-round

Usage principle
- Power-ups should sharpen the narrative, not replace it.


======================================================================
11. SCORING
======================================================================

Final placement uses a 105-point scorecard.

Tier 1: Performance (35 pts)
- Net Return: 20 pts
- Alpha vs BALL: 15 pts

Tier 2: Capital Discipline (40 pts)
- Drawdown Control: 15 pts
- Margin Discipline: 15 pts
- Funding Efficiency: 10 pts

Tier 3: Market Craft (30 pts)
- Crisis Alpha: 10 pts
- Win Rate: 5 pts
- Sector Diversification: 5 pts
- Prediction Accuracy: 5 pts
- Prediction Participation: 5 pts

Scorecard principle
- Live leaderboard is not the final result.
- Final scoring rewards return quality, risk control, and macro judgment.


======================================================================
12. TIEBREAKERS
======================================================================

Tie-breakers apply in this order:
1. Higher ending net equity
2. Higher crisis alpha
3. Fewer margin breaches
4. Lower max drawdown
5. Earlier last-trade timestamp


======================================================================
13. GM OPERATING CHECKLIST
======================================================================

Before the event
- Confirm all teams can log in
- Confirm the central display is visible
- Confirm the GM controls are working
- Confirm the derivatives tab and strategy builder are live

At the start of each round
- Announce the round name and macro theme
- Remind teams of any special rule changes
- Do not reveal the exact stocks or sectors impacted by the disruption

During the round
- Let silent storyline beats land through News
- Avoid adding random generic shocks unless you want a deliberate power-up
- Watch for margin stress and confusion around settlement

At the end of each round
- Let derivatives settle
- Allow the 5-second team-specific settlement window to play
- Move into the next round buffer and bulletin

For Round 5
- Keep the leaderboard hidden on all public displays

For the final round
- Let the settlement window play before teams fully absorb the ending board


======================================================================
14. PLAYER QUICK REFERENCE
======================================================================

Read the disruption like a macro clue, not a stock tip.

Ask:
- Which business models gain pricing power here?
- Which balance sheets get stressed?
- Which sectors depend on rates, energy, policy, logistics, or funding?
- Does price action confirm the thesis?

Risk reminders
- Shorts use collateral
- Later rounds punish leverage harder
- A hedge package may be safer than a naked directional position
- Round-expiry derivatives settle automatically

Best practice
- Enter with a thesis
- Size with discipline
- Re-check margin after every major move
- Use the settlement window to learn what actually paid off
